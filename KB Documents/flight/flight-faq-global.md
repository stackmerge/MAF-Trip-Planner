# Flight Booking FAQs — Global

---

## IDENTITY & DOCUMENTATION

### Q: What ID is accepted for domestic flights in India?
Aadhaar card, Passport, Voter ID, Driving License, PAN Card, or any government-issued photo ID. Aadhaar is most commonly used.

### Q: What ID is required for domestic flights in the USA?
A government-issued REAL ID-compliant driver's license, state ID, or US passport. As of May 7, 2025, standard (non-REAL ID) state IDs are no longer accepted at TSA checkpoints. Look for a star marking on your ID to confirm REAL ID compliance.

### Q: What documents are needed for international travel?
- Valid passport (check destination-specific validity requirements — many countries require 3–6 months validity beyond travel dates)
- Visa or electronic travel authorization (if applicable)
- Return/onward ticket (often required at immigration)
- Proof of accommodation (hotels, host details)
- Travel insurance (mandatory in some countries, e.g., Schengen area requires minimum €30,000 medical coverage)

### Q: What is ESTA, and do I need it?
ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization) is required for travelers from 42 Visa Waiver Program (VWP) countries visiting the USA. It costs $21, is valid for 2 years or until your passport expires, and must be applied for at least 72 hours before departure at esta.cbp.dhs.gov.

### Q: Do Indian passport holders need a visa for Singapore?
No — Indian passport holders can visit Singapore visa-free for up to 30 days (as of 2024). Confirm the latest rules before travel.

### Q: What is ETIAS (Europe)?
ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorization System) is a pre-travel authorization for non-EU nationals visiting Schengen Area countries. It costs €7 and is valid for 3 years. It is expected to launch in 2025. Check the official ETIAS website for the latest status before traveling.

## BAGGAGE

### Q: What is the baggage limit for carry-on liquids?
For all international flights and most domestic flights worldwide: liquids must be in containers of 100ml (3.4 oz) or less, placed in a single transparent 1-litre (quart-sized) zip-lock bag. One bag per passenger. Applies to all international departures from airports in India, the USA, Europe, the Middle East, and most of Asia-Pacific.

Exceptions: Medications, baby formula, and breast milk are generally exempt when declared at security.

### Q: Can I carry food on a domestic flight?
**India:** Yes. Solid home-cooked food is allowed. Liquids/gravies must comply with the 100ml rule on international routes.
**USA:** Solid foods are generally permitted in carry-on and checked bags. TSA recommends placing food items in clear containers to speed up screening.
**Europe:** Solid foods allowed. Liquids follow the 100ml/1-litre bag rule.

### Q: What happens if my checked bag exceeds the weight limit?
You will be charged an overweight fee at the check-in counter. Fees vary:
- India domestic: ₹300–₹600 per extra kg
- USA domestic: $100–$200 for bags 51–70 lbs (23–32kg)
- Europe (LCC): €10–€25 per extra kg (paid at airport is usually more expensive than pre-purchasing online)
- International: $50–$200+ per extra piece or per kg depending on the airline and route

## CHECK-IN & BOARDING

### Q: What is DigiYatra (India)?
DigiYatra is a free facial recognition-based paperless boarding system at major Indian airports. Link your Aadhaar and boarding pass via the DigiYatra app to skip manual ID checks. Available at DEL, BOM, HYD, BLR, MAA, CCU, PNQ, and more.

### Q: What is TSA PreCheck (USA)?
TSA PreCheck is a US government trusted traveler program that offers expedited security screening at 200+ US airports. Benefits include no removal of shoes, laptops, liquids, or belts at security. Cost: $85 for 5 years. Apply via TSA's website or an enrollment center.

### Q: What is Global Entry (USA)?
Global Entry allows pre-approved, low-risk travelers to expedite US customs clearance upon arrival from abroad using automated kiosks. Cost: $100 for 5 years. Includes TSA PreCheck. Apply via the CBP Trusted Traveler Programs website.

## BOOKINGS & PAYMENTS

### Q: What is a PNR number?
PNR (Passenger Name Record) is a 6–10 character alphanumeric code that uniquely identifies your booking. Use it to check booking status, web check-in, and modify/cancel flights on the airline's website or app. This applies globally, though international airlines may also refer to it as a booking reference or confirmation number.

### Q: Can I book a flight for someone else?
Yes, globally. Enter the passenger's name exactly as it appears on their government-issued ID or passport. Your contact details will be used for communication.

### Q: How does UPI payment work (India)?
Select UPI at checkout. Enter your UPI ID (e.g., name@upi) or scan the QR code. Confirm payment in your UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, etc.). Booking is confirmed instantly on successful payment.

### Q: What is Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) for flights?
Many booking platforms and airlines now offer BNPL financing:
- **India:** EMI via HDFC, ICICI, Axis Bank credit cards (no-cost EMI on select routes)
- **USA:** Affirm, Klarna, Uplift (available on most major airline and OTA sites)
- **Europe:** Klarna, Afterpay on select platforms
- **Global OTAs:** Booking.com, Expedia, Kayak partner with Klarna/Affirm in eligible countries

### Q: Is GST refundable on cancelled tickets (India)?
Yes. GST is fully refundable if you cancel before departure and are eligible for a refund.

## FLIGHT DISRUPTIONS & PASSENGER RIGHTS

### Q: What happens if my flight is delayed over 6 hours (India)?
Under DGCA rules, the airline must offer: a full refund or re-booking on the next available flight at no extra charge. Meals and refreshments must be provided for delays over 2 hours.

### Q: What are my rights if my EU flight is cancelled or significantly delayed?
Under EU Regulation EC 261/2004, if you are departing from an EU airport (or arriving at one on an EU carrier), and your flight is cancelled with less than 14 days notice or delayed by 3+ hours due to the airline's fault, you are entitled to:
- Compensation: €250–€600 based on flight distance
- Choice of: full refund or re-routing
- Right to care: meals, refreshments, hotel (if overnight delay)

### Q: What are my rights if a US flight is cancelled?
Under DOT rules, if an airline cancels a flight, you are entitled to a full cash refund — not just a travel credit — to your original payment method. Compensation for delays is not federally mandated in the USA (unlike the EU), but airlines may offer vouchers or rebooking at their discretion.

### Q: What is denied boarding compensation?
| Region | Compensation |
|--------|-------------|
| India (DGCA) | ₹5,000 (alt. flight within 1 hour) to ₹10,000 (delay > 1 hour) |
| EU (EC 261) | €250–€600 depending on flight distance |
| USA (DOT) | 200% of one-way fare (up to $775) for delays of 1–2 hrs; 400% (up to $1,550) for longer delays |

### Q: How do I earn miles on partner flights?
When you book a code-share or partner flight, enter your frequent flyer number from your home airline (or the operating airline's program) during booking. Miles typically credit within 3–7 business days of travel. You may need to request retro-credit manually if miles don't appear.

### Q: What is the infant fare for flights?
**India Domestic:** 10% of adult base fare + ₹450 airport tax. Infant (under 2) travels on a lap.
**International (most carriers):** 10% of adult fare + applicable taxes. A separate seat = child fare.
**USA:** Domestic infant-in-lap is free on most carriers. International infant fares vary (10–20% of adult fare).
