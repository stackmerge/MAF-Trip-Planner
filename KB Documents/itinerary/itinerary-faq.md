# Itinerary Planning FAQs

---

## General Planning

### Q: How far in advance should I plan my trip?
- Domestic peak season (Diwali, Christmas, New Year, summer): 2–3 months in advance
  (flights and hotels fill up fast)
- Domestic off-season: 2–4 weeks is usually fine
- International (Dubai, Singapore): 4–6 weeks for good fares and hotel rates
- International peak (Maldives Nov–Jan, Thailand Dec–Feb): 2–3 months for good resorts

### Q: What should I book in advance?
**India:**
- Taj Mahal: Online ticket mandatory (₹50 Indian, ₹1,100 foreign); avoid Monday (closed)
- Alleppey houseboat: Book 2–3 weeks ahead in peak season
- Kerala houseboat (luxury): 1–2 months ahead in Nov–Jan
- Rohtang Pass permit: Online booking opens 1 day prior; sells out fast
- Desert camps (Jaisalmer): 2–4 weeks ahead in Oct–Feb

**International:**
- Maldives resort + seaplane: 1–2 months ahead in peak season (Dec–Jan, Jul–Aug)
- Universal Studios Singapore: Purchase tickets online to skip queues
- Burj Khalifa At the Top: Pre-book 1–2 weeks ahead to avoid sellouts and
  save AED 30–50 vs at-the-door pricing
- Thailand island-hopping tours: 3–5 days ahead in peak season (Dec–Feb)
- Sri Lanka Kandy–Ella train: Book 2nd class observation car 4–6 weeks ahead;
  it sells out weeks in advance

### Q: Can I use UPI abroad?
UPI works in UAE, Singapore, Malaysia, France, UK, Bhutan, Nepal, and some other
countries via BHIM app and partner payment networks. However, acceptance is still
patchy — carry a travel forex card (HDFC, Thomas Cook, BookMyForex) or Wise card
as backup. Cash in local currency is still essential for street food markets, local
transport, and smaller towns.

## India-Specific FAQs

### Q: What is the best way to get around Rajasthan?
Hire a private cab + driver for the full circuit (Jaipur–Ajmer–Pushkar–Jodhpur–
Jaisalmer–Udaipur). Cost: ₹12,000–₹18,000 for 7–8 days. More comfortable and flexible
than trains for a family.

### Q: Is Goa good in August?
August is monsoon season. Most beach shacks are closed, and roads can flood. However,
Goa is lush and green, prices are low (40–50% cheaper), and Dudhsagar Falls is at its
most spectacular. Not recommended for beach seekers; good for budget travelers who enjoy
nature.

### Q: Is Kerala safe for solo women travelers?
Yes — Kerala consistently ranks among the safest Indian states for women travelers.
Kochi, Munnar, and Alleppey are all well-touristed with good infrastructure.

### Q: How do I handle Indian train bookings?
- Book via IRCTC (irctc.co.in) or IRCTC Rail Connect app
- Tatkal quota: Opens 1 day before departure at 10 AM (AC classes)
  — costs ₹400–₹500 extra
- Tourist quota: Available at railway stations for foreign nationals
- Popular trains: Rajdhani (Delhi–Mumbai/Kolkata), Shatabdi (intercity),
  Vande Bharat (modern semi-high speed)

### Q: What is a realistic daily budget for a family of 4 in Goa?
- Budget: ₹5,000–₹8,000/day (OYO stay, shack meals, local transport)
- Mid-range: ₹12,000–₹20,000/day (3–4 star hotel, restaurants, activities)
- Luxury: ₹35,000–₹60,000+/day (5-star resort, fine dining, private transfers)

## International Destination FAQs

### Q: Which international destination is easiest for first-time travelers from India?
**Singapore or Malaysia** are the most beginner-friendly:
- Malaysia: Visa-free entry, English widely spoken, familiar food (South Indian
  restaurants everywhere), short 4–5hr flight from most Indian metros
- Singapore: Free e-visa, extremely clean and safe, excellent public transport,
  UPI works via PayNow, very well-organized for tourists
Thailand is also very popular and budget-friendly but does require more planning for
island-hopping logistics.

### Q: Is Dubai worth it for a family, or is it too expensive?
Dubai is mid-to-expensive but manageable with planning. A family of 3 (2 adults + 1 child)
can do 5 days reasonably for ₹1,20,000–₹1,50,000 all-inclusive excluding flights.
Key savings tips: use the Metro instead of cabs, visit Dubai Mall + Fountain (free),
limit to 1–2 paid attractions per day (Burj Khalifa, desert safari, or a water park),
and eat at least one meal a day in the Gold Souk / Satwa area (cheap and excellent).

### Q: What is the best season to visit Bali?
April–October is the dry season and ideal for Bali. November–March is the wet season
with heavy afternoon rains — still doable but outdoor plans can get disrupted.
Peak tourist season is July–August (European summer holidays), which means higher
prices and more crowds. April–June and September–October offer the best combination
of good weather, manageable crowds, and reasonable pricing.

### Q: How do I travel between islands in the Maldives?
- Seaplane: Scenic and fast (20–45 min to most atolls); USD 200–500 return;
  only operates during daylight hours (roughly 6 AM–5:30 PM) — plan arrival/departure
  flights accordingly
- Speedboat: Slower but cheaper (USD 50–100); available for some atolls
- Local ferry: Cheapest option (MVR 10–30); runs fixed routes; only practical if
  staying on a local island like Maafushi rather than a resort
- Most resorts arrange transfers when you book — always confirm at booking time

### Q: Is Sri Lanka a budget-friendly destination?
Yes — Sri Lanka is one of the most affordable international destinations for Indian
travelers. A couple can have a comfortable mid-range 10-day trip for ₹80,000–₹1,20,000
all-inclusive excluding flights. Budget travelers can do it for less. Highlights:
very cheap street food (₹60–₹150/meal equivalent), reasonable hotel prices, low
attraction entry fees, and low internal transport costs (tuk-tuks and trains).

### Q: Can vegetarians eat well in Thailand and Bali?
**Thailand:** Vegetarian options exist but require care — Thai cooking often uses
fish sauce and shrimp paste in dishes that appear vegetarian. Look for "Jay" (เจ)
restaurants (marked with a yellow flag) which are strictly vegan/vegetarian.
Major cities and tourist hubs have good vegetarian options.

**Bali:** Much more vegetarian-friendly. Ubud especially has a huge range of plant-based,
vegan, and vegetarian restaurants. Hindu-majority Bali has many traditional dishes that
are naturally vegetarian (though pork is prominent in non-veg options). Indian restaurants
are also found in Seminyak and Canggu.

### Q: Do I need travel insurance for international trips?
Strongly recommended for all international travel:
- **Medical coverage** is the most important: treatment costs in Dubai, Singapore,
  and the Maldives can be extremely high (a hospital stay can cost USD 2,000–10,000+)
- **Trip cancellation / delay coverage**: useful for monsoon-affected destinations
  (Thailand, Bali) and long-haul connections
- **Lost baggage**: standard but helpful on multi-leg trips
- Recommended providers: HDFC Ergo, ICICI Lombard, Tata AIG, Bajaj Allianz
  (buy domestic plans for international travel); Policybazaar for comparison
- Maldives and Bali especially: medical evacuation to a proper hospital can cost
  USD 10,000–50,000 — insurance is non-negotiable

### Q: What power adapter do I need for international destinations?

| Destination   | Plug Type | Voltage |
|---------------|-----------|---------|
| UAE (Dubai)   | Type G (3-pin UK) | 220–240V |
| Singapore     | Type G (3-pin UK) | 220–240V |
| Thailand      | Type A/B/C (mixed)| 220V     |
| Maldives      | Type G / Type D   | 220–240V |
| Sri Lanka     | Type D/G          | 230V     |
| Malaysia      | Type G (3-pin UK) | 240V     |
| Bali/Indonesia| Type C/F (European round)| 220V |

A universal travel adapter covers all of the above. Most modern phone and laptop chargers
handle 100–240V automatically — check the label on your charger brick.

### Q: How do I handle money in international destinations?
- **Carry a mix:** forex card + some local currency cash + an international debit/credit card
- **Best forex cards:** HDFC Regalia Forex, Niyo Global, BookMyForex, Wise (multi-currency)
- **ATMs abroad:** Use bank ATMs (not standalone kiosks) to avoid high fees;
  withdraw larger amounts to minimize per-withdrawal fees
- **Currency exchange:** Exchange at airport kiosks only for small emergency amounts;
  better rates at city money changers or pre-purchased forex cards
- **Avoid:** Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) at POS machines — always pay in local
  currency, not INR, to avoid 3–5% hidden conversion markup

### Q: Is it safe to travel to Bali / Thailand alone?
Both are considered very safe for solo travelers, including solo women. Tourism is a
massive industry in both destinations — locals are accustomed to and reliant on tourist
visitors. Standard precautions apply: don't leave drinks unattended, use official taxis
or Grab, avoid deserted beaches alone at night. Ubud (Bali) and Chiang Mai (Thailand)
are especially popular and well-established for solo travel.
