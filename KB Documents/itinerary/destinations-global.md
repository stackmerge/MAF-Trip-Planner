# 🇮🇳 India

## Goa

### Overview
- Best for: Beaches, nightlife, Portuguese heritage, water sports, seafood
- Ideal duration: 4–6 days
- Best season: November–February
- Nearest airport: GOI (Goa International / Manohar International Airport, Dabolim)
- Local transport: Rented scooter (₹300–₹500/day), cab (₹200–₹800/trip),
  auto-rickshaw (₹80–₹200)

### Must-Visit Attractions
- Baga Beach & Calangute Beach (North Goa): lively, water sports, beach shacks
- Anjuna Beach: flea market every Wednesday, bohemian vibe
- Palolem Beach (South Goa): crescent-shaped, calm, best for swimming
- Basilica of Bom Jesus (Old Goa): UNESCO World Heritage Site, St. Francis Xavier's tomb
- Se Cathedral (Old Goa): one of the largest churches in Asia
- Dudhsagar Falls: 4-tiered waterfall, 60km from Panaji; visit Oct–May only
- Fontainhas (Panaji Latin Quarter): heritage walk, pastel houses, galleries
- Spice Plantation Tour: Ponda area; ₹400–₹600 per person including lunch

### Food Recommendations
- Must eat: Fish curry rice, prawn balchão, bebinca (Goan dessert), feni (local spirit)
- Famous shacks: Brittos (Baga), Infantaria (Calangute), Fisherman's Wharf (South Goa)
- Budget meal: ₹150–₹400 | Mid-range: ₹500–₹1,200 | Fine dining: ₹2,000+

### Hidden Gem
- Cabo de Rama Fort (South Goa): ruins of a Portuguese fort with stunning sea views,
  almost no crowds, free entry

## Rajasthan (Jaipur–Udaipur–Jodhpur Circuit)

### Overview
- Best for: Heritage, palaces, desert, culture, photography
- Ideal duration: 7–10 days for circuit
- Best season: October–March
- Airports: JAI (Jaipur), UDR (Udaipur), JDH (Jodhpur)

### Jaipur (Pink City)
- Amber Fort & Palace (advance online booking recommended): ₹100–₹500 entry
- City Palace: royal residence + museum; ₹200–₹700
- Hawa Mahal: iconic facade; ₹50
- Jantar Mantar (UNESCO): astronomical instruments; ₹50–₹200
- Johari Bazaar: gems, jewelry, traditional textiles
- Hidden Gem: Panna Meena Ka Kund — stunning stepwell, few tourists, free entry

### Udaipur (City of Lakes)
- City Palace: largest palace complex in Rajasthan; ₹250–₹400
- Lake Pichola boat ride: sunset cruise to Jag Mandir; ₹400–₹700
- Jagdish Temple: 17th century Hindu temple, free entry
- Saheliyon Ki Bari: garden, fountains; ₹10
- Hidden Gem: Sajjangarh (Monsoon Palace) at sunset — panoramic views, uncrowded

### Jodhpur (Blue City)
- Mehrangarh Fort: one of India's largest forts; ₹100–₹600 with audio guide
- Jaswant Thada: white marble cenotaph; ₹30
- Ghanta Ghar Clock Tower & Sardar Market: best bazaar in Jodhpur
- Hidden Gem: Toorji Ka Jhalra (stepwell) — beautifully restored, free, best at golden hour

## Kerala

### Overview
- Best for: Backwaters, beaches, Ayurveda, tea/spice estates, wildlife
- Ideal duration: 6–8 days
- Best season: October–February (post-monsoon, lush and pleasant)
- Airports: COK (Kochi), TRV (Thiruvananthapuram), CCJ (Calicut/Kozhikode)

### Kochi (Cochin)
- Fort Kochi: Chinese fishing nets, colonial architecture, art galleries
- Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace): Kerala murals; ₹5 entry
- Jewish Synagogue (Paradesi): 16th century; ₹10; closed Saturdays
- Kerala Kathakali performance: evening shows; ₹200–₹400
- Hidden Gem: Vypeen Island — ferry from Fort Kochi (₹5), quiet beaches, no crowds

### Alleppey (Alappuzha) — Backwaters
- Houseboat stay: overnight on backwaters; ₹5,000–₹25,000/night
- Punnamada Lake: Nehru Trophy Boat Race venue (held annually in August)
- Kuttanad: below sea-level paddy fields, canoe village tours; ₹300–₹600

### Munnar
- Tea Museum (KDHP): how tea is processed; ₹75
- Eravikulam National Park: Nilgiri tahr (endangered mountain goat); ₹125
- Top Station: highest point, panoramic Western Ghats views
- Hidden Gem: Mattupetty Dam early morning — mist, silence, no tourist buses yet

### Wayanad
- Edakkal Caves: prehistoric rock engravings; ₹30; 1.5km trek
- Chembra Peak trek: heart-shaped lake at top; ₹300–₹500 with guide
- Pookode Lake: ₹30 entry, paddle boats; surrounded by forest

## Himachal Pradesh (Manali–Shimla)

### Overview
- Best for: Mountains, snow, adventure sports, scenic drives, honeymoons
- Ideal duration: 6–8 days
- Best season: March–June (summer), Dec–Jan (snow)
- Airports: SLV (Shimla — limited), KUU (Kullu–Manali);
  many fly to DEL or CHD (Chandigarh) then drive
- Road trip: Delhi → Shimla (350km, 8–9hrs) | Shimla → Manali (260km, 8hrs)

### Shimla
- Mall Road & The Ridge: colonial promenade, Christ Church
- Jakhu Temple: Hanuman temple, 2.5km hike or ropeway; ₹100 ropeway
- Kufri: 17km from Shimla, skiing in winter; pony rides, Mini Zoo
- Toy Train (Kalka–Shimla): UNESCO Heritage railway; ₹35–₹505 depending on class

### Manali
- Solang Valley: skiing, zorbing, paragliding; ₹500–₹2,000/activity
- Rohtang Pass (3,978m): snow even in June; permit required (₹500 online)
- Hadimba Devi Temple: unique pagoda-style temple; free
- Old Manali: cafes, hippie culture, backpacker scene
- Spiti Valley side trip (2–3 days extra): Moon landscape, Key Monastery
- Hidden Gem: Naggar Castle — 15km from Manali, hilltop, almost no crowds, stunning valley views

## Andaman & Nicobar Islands

### Overview
- Best for: Beaches, snorkeling, scuba diving, pristine nature
- Ideal duration: 5–7 days
- Best season: November–April (avoid monsoon June–September)
- Nearest airport: IXZ (Port Blair)
- Inter-island transport: Government ferry or private speedboat

### Port Blair
- Cellular Jail (Kala Pani): colonial prison, now national memorial; ₹30 Indian, ₹100 foreign
- Light & Sound show at Cellular Jail: evenings; ₹50
- Corbyn's Cove Beach: closest beach to Port Blair, calm waters

### Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep)
- Radhanagar Beach (Beach No. 7): rated among Asia's best; free entry
- Elephant Beach: snorkeling, glass-bottom boat rides; ₹500–₹800 activity packages
- Scuba diving: ₹3,500–₹5,000/dive (PADI certified centres available)

### Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep)
- Bharatpur & Laxmanpur beaches: calm, ideal for swimming and snorkeling
- Natural Bridge (Howrah Bridge): rock formation at low tide; free
- Hidden Gem: Sunset point at Laxmanpur Beach 2 — uncrowded, stunning

## Dubai, UAE

### Overview
- Best for: Shopping, luxury experiences, desert adventures, family attractions
- Ideal duration: 4–6 days
- Best season: November–March (Oct–Apr pleasant; May–Sep extremely hot)
- Nearest airport: DXB (Dubai International Airport)
- Visa: Required for Indian passport holders; approx ₹5,000–₹8,000; 3–5 working days
- Currency: AED (1 AED ≈ ₹23)
- Local transport: Metro (AED 3–8/trip), cab (AED 25–35 for 10km), Nol card recommended

### Must-Visit Attractions
- Burj Khalifa At the Top: world's tallest building; pre-book online (AED 149–249/person)
- Dubai Mall + Dubai Fountain: fountain show free every 30 min after 6 PM
- Dubai Aquarium & Underwater Zoo: AED 100/adult, AED 75/child
- Gold Souk & Spice Souk, Deira: traditional markets; free entry; best for bargain shopping
- Al Fahidi Historical District: free; heritage walk; old Dubai feel
- Abra (traditional boat) crossing: AED 1 — cheapest and most iconic Dubai experience
- Palm Jumeirah Monorail: AED 25 return
- Dubai Frame: 150m picture frame structure with glass bridge; AED 50/person
- Desert Safari: dune bashing + camel ride + BBQ dinner; AED 100–200/person

### Theme Parks & Water Parks
- IMG Worlds of Adventure: AED 275–345/person; Marvel, Cartoon Network zones
- Dubai Parks & Resorts (Motiongate, Bollywood Parks, Legoland): AED 250–350/person
- Atlantis Aquaventure Water Park: AED 245–295/person

### Food Recommendations
- Must try: Shawarma (AED 5–10), Al Harees, Luqaimat (sweet dumplings), Arabic mezze
- Budget: Ravi Restaurant, Satwa — legendary Pakistani-Indian food; AED 30 for 3
- Mid-range: Mall food courts, global chains (AED 80–150 for two)
- Fine dining: Pierchic, Nobu, Zuma (AED 300–600/person)

### Hidden Gem
- Al Quoz & Alserkal Avenue: Dubai's art district — galleries, indie cafes, street art;
  free to walk; weekends are best; very few tourists

### Practical Tips
- Metro is affordable and air-conditioned; pick up a Nol card on arrival
- Friday is the weekly holiday — some souks and offices closed in the morning
- Ramadan timings significantly change operating hours — plan accordingly
- Dress modestly in souks, malls, and religious sites
- UPI works at select points; carry a forex card (HDFC, BookMyForex, Wise) as backup

## Singapore

### Overview
- Best for: Food, city culture, family attractions, cleanliness, transit hub for SE Asia
- Ideal duration: 4–5 days
- Best season: Year-round (tropical); Feb–April slightly drier
- Nearest airport: SIN (Changi Airport — consistently rated world's best)
- Visa: Free e-visa for Indian passport holders; apply online 3–7 working days ahead
- Currency: SGD (1 SGD ≈ ₹63)
- Local transport: MRT (efficient, cheap); EZ-Link card recommended

### Must-Visit Attractions
- Gardens by the Bay: Supertrees, Cloud Forest, Flower Dome; SGD 28–53/person
- Marina Bay Sands SkyPark: observation deck; SGD 26
- Sentosa Island: Universal Studios Singapore (SGD 83/adult), free beaches on island
- Orchard Road: premier shopping street
- Little India & Arab Street: vibrant cultural enclaves, street food, textiles
- Chinatown: heritage streets, hawker centres, Sri Mariamman Temple (free)
- Singapore Zoo & Night Safari: SGD 48–55/adult

### Food Recommendations
- Must eat: Chicken Rice, Laksa, Chilli Crab, Char Kway Teow, Kaya Toast
- Hawker centres: Newton Food Centre, Maxwell Food Centre (mains SGD 3–8)
- Mid-range: SGD 40–80 for two
- Fine dining: Odette, Restaurant André (Michelin-starred; SGD 300–500/person)

### Hidden Gem
- Pulau Ubin Island: 10-min bumboat (SGD 4) from Changi Point Ferry Terminal;
  old kampong village life, cycling trails, mangroves; almost no tourists

### Practical Tips
- UPI payments accepted at many hawker stalls via PayNow
- Changi Airport's Jewel (waterfall mall) worth visiting even in transit
- Grab (local Uber equivalent) is reliable and affordable

## Thailand (Bangkok + Phuket / Krabi)

### Overview
- Best for: Street food, temples, nightlife, islands, budget travel
- Ideal duration: 7–10 days (Bangkok 3–4 days + islands 3–5 days)
- Best season: November–April (cool and dry)
- Airports: BKK (Suvarnabhumi, Bangkok), HKT (Phuket), KBV (Krabi)
- Visa: Visa on arrival (₹0–₹2,500); e-visa also available
- Currency: THB (1 THB ≈ ₹2.4)
- Local transport: Tuk-tuk, BTS Skytrain (Bangkok), longtail boat (islands)

### Bangkok
- Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha): THB 500
- Wat Pho: reclining Buddha; THB 200
- Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn): THB 100; stunning from the river at sunset
- Chatuchak Weekend Market: 8,000+ stalls; free entry; best Sat–Sun
- Khao San Road: backpacker hub, street food, nightlife
- Chao Phraya River express boat: THB 40–180
- Floating markets (Amphawa or Damnoen Saduak): day trip; THB 300–500

### Phuket
- Patong Beach: lively, jet ski, nightlife on Bangla Road
- Phi Phi Islands day trip: THB 800–1,500 (boat tour)
- Big Buddha: 45m marble statue, hilltop; free
- Old Phuket Town: Sino-Portuguese architecture, street art

### Krabi
- Railay Beach: accessible only by longtail boat (THB 150); stunning limestone cliffs
- Four Islands Tour: THB 600–900; snorkeling and kayaking
- Tiger Cave Temple: 1,237 steps to panoramic views; free

### Food Recommendations
- Must eat: Pad Thai, Som Tam, Tom Yum, Mango Sticky Rice, Green/Red Curry
- Street food meal: THB 50–100 (₹120–₹240)
- Mid-range restaurant: THB 300–600 for two

### Hidden Gem
- Koh Lanta (near Krabi): quieter than Phuket, beautiful beaches,
  laid-back vibe, fewer crowds; best Nov–Mar

### Practical Tips
- Carry small THB notes for street food and tuk-tuks
- Dress modestly at temples — cover knees and shoulders
- Negotiate tuk-tuk prices before getting in

## Maldives

### Overview
- Best for: Luxury beach holidays, honeymoons, snorkeling, complete relaxation
- Ideal duration: 4–6 days
- Best season: November–April (dry season, excellent underwater visibility)
- Nearest airport: MLE (Velana International Airport, Malé)
- Visa: Free on arrival; 30-day stamp
- Currency: MVR (US Dollar widely accepted; 1 USD ≈ ₹83)
- Local transport: Seaplane (USD 200–500 return), speedboat (USD 50–100),
  local ferry (budget; MVR 10–30)

### Must-Visit Experiences
- Overwater bungalow stay: mid-range ₹25,000–₹50,000/night;
  luxury ₹80,000–₹3,00,000+/night
- Snorkeling & scuba diving: house reef snorkeling free at most resorts;
  diving USD 60–100/dive
- Dolphin watching cruise: USD 30–60/person; best at sunset
- Bioluminescent beach: glowing plankton visible on dark nights on some islands
- Malé city tour: Grand Friday Mosque, fish market, National Museum
- Local island stay (Maafushi, Dhigurah): guesthouses from USD 60/night

### Food Recommendations
- Resort dining: full board recommended (USD 80–150/person/day)
- Must try: Mas Huni (tuna & coconut breakfast), Garudhiya (fish broth soup),
  Rihaakuru (fish paste)
- Local islands: small cafes serve fish curry and short eats for MVR 20–50

### Hidden Gem
- Fuvahmulah Island: tiger sharks and thresher sharks year-round,
  whale sharks in season; a serious divers' paradise; largely unknown to tourists

### Practical Tips
- Book resort + seaplane transfers together — seaplanes only operate during daylight
- Travel insurance strongly recommended (medical evacuation can be very expensive)
- Alcohol only available at resort islands, not local islands

## Sri Lanka

### Overview
- Best for: Culture, wildlife, tea estates, beaches, affordability
- Ideal duration: 7–10 days
- Best season: December–March (west/south coast); May–September (east coast)
- Airports: CMB (Colombo — Bandaranaike), HRI (Mattala — south)
- Visa: ETA required; USD 20–35; apply at eta.gov.lk; usually immediate approval
- Currency: LKR (1 USD ≈ LKR 320 approx)
- Local transport: Tuk-tuk, scenic train (Kandy–Ella is iconic), hired car+driver

### Must-Visit Attractions
- Sigiriya Rock Fortress: UNESCO site, ancient palace atop a 200m rock; USD 30
- Dambulla Cave Temple: 150+ Buddha statues; USD 10
- Kandy: Temple of the Tooth Relic; cultural capital; LKR 1,000
- Ella: Nine Arches Bridge, Little Adam's Peak hike, tea factory visits; mostly free
- Yala National Park: leopard sightings common; safari USD 60–100/person
- Mirissa: whale watching (blue whales, Dec–Apr); USD 30–50/person
- Galle Fort: colonial Dutch fort, cafes, boutiques; free entry

### Food Recommendations
- Must eat: Rice & curry, Kottu Roti, Hoppers (string/egg), Pol Sambol, Wood Apple juice
- Street meal: LKR 200–500 (₹60–₹140)
- Mid-range restaurant: LKR 1,500–3,000 for two
- Seafood on the coast: LKR 1,000–2,000 for a full fish meal

### Hidden Gem
- Knuckles Mountain Range: UNESCO-listed biodiversity hotspot near Kandy;
  spectacular trekking, mist, waterfalls; almost no international tourists

### Practical Tips
- Scenic train Kandy to Ella (7hrs): book 2nd class observation car well in advance
  (sells out weeks ahead)
- Indian debit/credit cards work at most ATMs; carry USD as backup
- Very safe for solo and family travelers

## Malaysia (Kuala Lumpur + Langkawi / Penang)

### Overview
- Best for: Food, multicultural culture, islands, rainforest, budget-friendly
- Ideal duration: 6–9 days
- Best season: May–July and Dec–Jan (varies by region)
- Airports: KUL (KLIA, Kuala Lumpur), LGK (Langkawi), PEN (Penang)
- Visa: Visa-free for Indian passport holders (up to 30 days)
- Currency: MYR (1 MYR ≈ ₹19)
- Local transport: Grab, LRT/MRT (KL), ferry (Langkawi/Penang)

### Kuala Lumpur
- Petronas Twin Towers: skybridge + observation deck; MYR 85/adult; book online
- Batu Caves: Hindu temple complex, 272 steps, giant Murugan statue; free
- Central Market (Pasar Seni): handicrafts, batik, souvenirs
- Bukit Bintang: shopping, nightlife, Jalan Alor street food
- Menara KL Tower: observation deck; MYR 52/adult

### Langkawi
- Cable Car & Sky Bridge: panoramic views; MYR 55
- Kilim Karst Geoforest Park: mangrove boat tour; MYR 100–150/person
- Duty-free shopping: alcohol, chocolates, perfumes significantly cheaper than mainland
- Cenang Beach: main beach strip, water sports and beach bars

### Penang (George Town)
- UNESCO Heritage Zone: street art, clan jetties, Peranakan mansions; free to walk
- Kek Lok Si Temple: largest Buddhist temple in Southeast Asia; free (donations)
- Penang Hill: funicular railway + panoramic views; MYR 30 return

### Food Recommendations
- Must eat: Char Kway Teow, Nasi Lemak, Roti Canai, Laksa, Satay, Cendol
- Hawker meal: MYR 5–15 (₹95–₹285)
- Mid-range: MYR 40–80 for two
- Indian food: Bangsar and Brickfields (KL) for excellent South Indian cuisine

### Hidden Gem
- Taman Negara National Park: one of the world's oldest rainforests (130 million years);
  3–4hr from KL; canopy walkway, night jungle walks, river cruises; very few tourists

### Practical Tips
- Visa-free — easiest international destination from India
- Grab is much cheaper than taxis; always use the app
- Carry MYR cash for hawker centres and smaller towns

## Bali, Indonesia

### Overview
- Best for: Culture, temples, rice terraces, surf, wellness, budget luxury
- Ideal duration: 7–10 days
- Best season: April–October (dry season)
- Nearest airport: DPS (Ngurah Rai International Airport, Denpasar)
- Visa: Visa on arrival (USD 35 for 30 days); extendable once
- Currency: IDR (1 USD ≈ IDR 16,000)
- Local transport: Hired driver (USD 40–60/day recommended),
  scooter rental (IDR 60,000–80,000/day), Grab (South Bali only)

### Must-Visit Areas & Attractions
- Ubud: Monkey Forest (IDR 80,000), Tegallalang Rice Terraces (IDR 15,000),
  Ubud Palace, art galleries, cooking classes
- Tanah Lot Temple: iconic sea temple at sunset; IDR 60,000
- Uluwatu Temple: clifftop temple + Kecak fire dance at sunset; IDR 50,000
- Seminyak & Canggu: beach clubs, surf, cafes, boutiques (South Bali)
- Nusa Penida island day trip: Kelingking Beach, Angel's Billabong, Broken Beach;
  speedboat IDR 150,000 return
- Mount Batur sunrise trek: active volcano; IDR 300,000–500,000 with guide

### Food Recommendations
- Must eat: Nasi Goreng, Babi Guling (pork), Bebek Betutu (duck), Satay, Lawar
- Warung (local restaurant) meal: IDR 25,000–60,000 (₹125–₹300)
- Mid-range: IDR 150,000–350,000 for two

### Hidden Gem
- Sidemen Valley: lush, quiet, traditional Balinese village life with spectacular rice
  terraces and zero tourist crowds; great alternative to Ubud; stay in a homestay

### Practical Tips
- Always carry small IDR cash — card acceptance is inconsistent
- Sarong required at all temples (usually provided free or IDR 10,000)
- Hire a driver for longer distances rather than riding a scooter
- Travel insurance highly recommended
