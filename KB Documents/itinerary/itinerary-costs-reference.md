## Packing Guide by Destination

### Beach — India (Goa, Andaman, Kerala Coast)
- Lightweight clothes, swimwear, flip-flops, sunscreen SPF 50+, sunglasses,
  waterproof bag, insect repellent, beach towel

### Mountains — India (Manali, Shimla, Darjeeling)
- Thermal innerwear, heavy jacket, waterproof trekking shoes, woollen cap,
  gloves, sunscreen, lip balm, portable charger (cold drains batteries)

### Heritage Circuit — India (Rajasthan, Agra, Delhi)
- Cotton/linen breathable clothes (Oct–Mar is fine, Apr–Sep is very hot),
  comfortable walking shoes, scarf (for temples, dusty forts), hat/cap

### Dubai, UAE
- Light summer clothes year-round; modest clothing for souks, malls, and mosques
  (cover shoulders and knees); comfortable walking shoes; travel adapter (Type G plug);
  sunscreen SPF 50+; sunglasses

### Singapore & Malaysia
- Light summer clothes; compact umbrella or rain jacket (sudden tropical showers);
  comfortable walking shoes; EZ-Link/Touch 'n Go card; travel adapter (Type G plug)

### Thailand & Bali
- Light breathable clothes; modest cover-ups for temples (sarong often provided);
  flip-flops + one pair of walking shoes; reef-safe sunscreen; insect repellent;
  small cash wallet for markets and street food

### Maldives
- Swimwear, light cover-ups, reef-safe sunscreen, snorkel mask (personal optional),
  waterproof phone pouch, modest clothing for local island visits

### Sri Lanka
- Light cotton clothes; one light layer for hill country (Ella/Kandy gets cool at night);
  comfortable walking shoes for ruins; modest dress for temples; compact rain jacket

## Payment & Money Tips

### India
- UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) accepted almost everywhere
- ATMs widely available; inform your bank before travel to avoid card blocks
- Keep ₹500–₹1,000 cash for autos, small dhabas, and remote areas

### International
- UPI works in UAE, Singapore, Malaysia, France, UK (via BHIM and partner networks)
- Wise card: best for multi-currency; low conversion fees; works globally
- Forex cards: HDFC Regalia Forex, Thomas Cook, BookMyForex — pre-load before travel
- Always carry some local currency cash for street food, tuk-tuks, and markets
- Notify your home bank of travel dates to avoid international transaction blocks
- Credit cards (Visa/Mastercard) widely accepted at hotels, malls, and restaurants
  in all listed international destinations
